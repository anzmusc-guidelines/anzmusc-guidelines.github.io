Guideline Panel Members

Meet the expert clinicians, researchers, and consumer representatives who develop our evidence-based recommendations.

About Our Panel

The Australian Living Guideline is developed by a multidisciplinary panel of expert clinicians, researchers, and consumer representatives. Panel members are selected based on their expertise relevant to each specific recommendation topic.

SW
Dr Samuel Whittle
Rheumatologist
Monash University

Dr Whittle is the lead developer for the Australian Living Guidelines for Adult Inflammatory Arthritis and Juvenile Idiopathic Arthritis. He is an ANZMUSC Practitioner Fellow. He works as a consultant rheumatologist at the Queen Elizabeth Hospital in Adelaide.

Faciliitator
Recommendations: All recommendations
RB
Prof Rachelle Buchbinder
Rheumatologist
Monash University

Professor Buchbinder is a rheumatologist and epidemiologist. She chairs the Australian Living Guideline panel, and is Chair of the Executive Committee, NHMRC Centre of Research Excellence for the Australia and New Zealand Musculoskeletal (ANZMUSC) Clinical Trials Network. She is Coordinating Editor of Cochrane Musculoskeletal. She has been the Director of the Monash Department of Clinical Epidemiology since its inception in 2001 and a Professor in the Monash University Department of Epidemiology and Preventive Medicine since 2007. She is a rheumatologist and clinical epidemiologist who combines clinical practice with research in a wide range of multidisciplinary projects relating to arthritis and musculoskeletal conditions.

Rheumatologist
University of Sydney

Professor Lyn March is the Head of Rheumatology at Sydney’s Royal North Shore Hospital and Liggins Professor of Rheumatology and Musculoskeletal Epidemiology at Sydney University.She has been an active clinician and researcher for more than 25 years. CH
Prof Catherine Hill
Rheumatologist
University of Adelaide

Professor Catherine Hill is a clinical rheumatologist, epidemiologist and expert in evidence-based healthcare.She trained in clinical rheumatology and general medicine at TQEH/RAH, research fellowship and Masters of Science (Biostats & Epidemiology) at Boston University and MD (University of Adelaide).She leads the Rheumatology Research Group at the Basil Hetzel Institute with research expertise in population epidemiology, randomised clinical trials, qualitative research, biobanking and quality improvement. The Research Group has experts in epidemiology, statistics, biobanking, qualitative methods and evidence-based medicine. It is the South Australian hub of Australian Arthritis and Autoimmune Biobank (A3BC). She was awarded the 2022 Parr Prize for Rheumatology Research Excellence. It is awarded every three years for outstanding contribution by a ARA member to rheumatology research; judged on quality and impact of research.She is past President of Australian Rheumatology Association (2020-22); professional society of rheumatologists and rheumatology health professionals. She is Chair of the Education Committee of Asia Pacific League Against Rheumatism (APLAR).She has experience and expertise in health technology assessment having been Chair of South Australian Medicines Evaluation Panel (SAMEP; 2011-2023), member of Australian Committee for Prescription Medicine (TGA, 2004-2015) and member of Pharmaceutical Benefits Advisory Committee (2016-2023).She is a Board Member of the Australian and New Zealand Vasculitis Society as a founding Board Member. This is a collaborative organisation of physicians, scientists, and communities that focuses on research, education, and clinical practices in vasculitis.She is a Board Member of The Hospital Research Foundation (THRF). In addition, she chairs the Clinical Governance Committee of the THRF and sits on the THRG-Arthritis Board of Governors.As Chief Investigator of the North West Adelaide Health Study (NWAHS), she has instrumental in adding the musculoskeletal data collection to this study. Work from this study has been published as part of the Global Burden of Disease study published in the Lancet (2012), which identified musculoskeletal diseases one of the three most important diseases in terms of global disease burden. Prof Hill is part of a national OA trials collaborative (Monash University, University of Tasmania, University of Sydney, University of WA) with current and previous NHMRC funding of interventional clinical trials for knee and hand osteoarthritis. She has collaborations with CSIRO and University of SA for innovative trials of knee OA. Previous trials have been reported in JAMA and Ann Rheum Dis. Further to this work, she is part of the European League Against Rheumatism (EULAR) Hand OA classification criteria Working group.Giant Cell Arteritis is the most common vasculitis in the elderly that can lead to blindness and stroke. She established the South Australian Giant Cell Arteritis Registry in 2009 which has resulted in further knowledge about this condition in Australia for the first time and has resulted in both national and international collaborations; and the first Australian data regarding incidence and prevalence of this condition. She is involved in OMERACT (Outcome Measures in Rheumatology) which is an independent initiative of international health professionals interested in outcome measures in rheumatology since 2014. She co-chairs 4 working groups for OMERACT (PMR, glucocorticoid impact, hand OA, POLOS) and is also part of the Technical Advisory Group of OMERACT.

Recommendations: Lab monitoring
